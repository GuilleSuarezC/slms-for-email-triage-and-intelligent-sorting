# Data card — 35-class synthetic energy-sector email corpus

**File:** `email_dataset.csv` (~31 MB) · **Companion:** `metadata.csv` (per-email
generation metadata) · **Rows:** 20,000 · **Columns:** 22 (see
[`../examples/SCHEMA.md`](../examples/SCHEMA.md)) · **Separator:** `;` · **UTF-8**

## ⚠️ This data is 100% synthetic

Every message in this corpus was **generated by local Small Language Models**;
none of it corresponds to a real person, a real customer, or a real email
account. It is a *controlled benchmark*, not real-world data.

- **Names, addresses, phone numbers, DNI/NIE, IBAN-like numbers, CUPS/contract
  identifiers and email addresses that appear inside the messages are fabricated
  by the generator.** They are realistic-looking on purpose (to make the
  classification task non-trivial) but do not identify real individuals.
- Some fabricated email addresses use **real company domains** (e.g.
  `@iberdrola.com`, `@endesa.es`) purely as plausible sender/recipient decoration.
  These addresses are invented and are **not** real mailboxes; no affiliation
  with, or endorsement by, those companies is implied.
- No data from any real inbox, ticketing system or customer was used at any
  stage (thesis §1.7 "Project Scope and Limitations": *"Real customer email data
  is not used at any stage of the project."*).

If you nonetheless prefer not to redistribute realistic-looking synthetic PII,
you can regenerate an equivalent corpus yourself with the generator in
`../src/generation/` (requires a local LM Studio server).

## How it was generated

- **Generator:** `../src/generation/energy35C_email_dataset_generator_v11.py`,
  driving two locally hosted models through an **LM Studio** server (no cloud API):
  - `deepseek/deepseek-r1-0528-qwen3-8b`
  - `google/gemma-4-e4b`  *(note: this string is the local-runtime model alias as
    recorded by the tooling; it is not a verified official Google release name —
    the thesis flags this too.)*
- **Reproducibility of generation:** a base seed of **42** is fixed; each email is
  then produced with its own sampled parameters (temperature ≈ 0.65–1.05,
  `top_p`, `top_k`) and a per-email seed, **all recorded per row** (columns
  `temperature`, `top_p`, `top_k`, `seed`, `model_assigned`) and in `metadata.csv`.
  Generation is therefore *auditable* even though it uses stochastic sampling.
- **Design:** deliberate linguistic noise, multi-intent messages, ambiguity, and
  rare-category injection (columns `is_multi_intent`, `is_ambiguous`,
  `noise_level`, …).
- **Language mix:** ~15,946 Spanish + ~4,054 English messages. **Class labels are
  kept in Spanish** by design.

## Known limitations (from the thesis)

- A synthetic corpus **does not replace real data for production validation**;
  reported results characterise behaviour on this *specific* controlled benchmark
  and should not be read as a generalisable claim about SLM capability or as
  real-world performance (thesis §4 "Limitations of the Synthetic Corpus" and §6.4).
- Any distributional biases of the generating models are inherited by the corpus.
